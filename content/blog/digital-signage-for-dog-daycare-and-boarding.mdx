Dog daycare and boarding facilities are one of the most overlooked venues for digital signage — and one of the best.

Here's why: pet parents are *anxious*. They drop off their dogs and immediately start worrying. Is he playing? Is she eating? Is the staff paying attention? That anxiety is your first real opportunity for a screen to do useful work — by showing a live play-cam feed or a rotating slideshow of dogs enjoying your facility, you ease those worries the moment someone walks in the door.

But there's more than anxiety management. The average dog daycare client visits 2-4 times per week. Drop-off and pick-up happen every single visit. That's 4-8 interactions per week, each with a 5-15 minute wait at your front desk — sometimes longer on busy mornings.

Those waiting moments are exactly what digital signage was built for.

## Why Dog Daycares Are a Perfect Fit for Digital Signage

**Repeat visitors with consistent wait times.** A client who brings their dog three times a week is in your lobby 6 times a week. That's more exposure than most retail stores. Regular clients are also your warmest audience for upsell content — they already trust you.

**High anxiety, high attention.** Pet parents aren't scrolling their phones while waiting at your front desk (well, some are — but many are watching the play yard, scanning the room, or looking for reassurance that their dog is fine). A well-placed screen captures attention that would otherwise be wasted.

**Premium local demographics.** Dog ownership skews toward households with disposable income. In markets like Arlington, that means homeowners in their 30s and 40s who spend money on experiences, health, food, and lifestyle. That's a desirable audience for local advertisers.

**Natural visual environment.** Dogs are inherently watchable. A screen showing dogs playing in a daycare yard isn't an interruption — it's entertainment. That positive association extends to everything else you run alongside it.

## 6 Ways Dog Daycares Use Digital Signage

### 1. Live Play-Cam or Photo Slideshows

Nothing reduces pet-parent anxiety faster than seeing your dog.

A screen in your lobby showing live footage from your play yard — or a rotating slideshow of photos from the day — transforms the drop-off experience. Instead of leaving and wondering, clients leave reassured. That emotional payoff turns a transactional interaction into a trust-building moment.

For boarding facilities, this is even more powerful. When someone leaves their dog for a weekend and walks in on Monday to see photos of their pup playing, eating, and napping, you've created a loyal client.

You don't need a sophisticated camera system. A simple IP camera with a live feed to a lobby TV, managed through your PiAds playlist, does the job. Rotate it in between your other content — or dedicate one screen to it entirely.

### 2. Service Upsells

Your lobby screen is your best sales tool, and it works while your staff is focused on dogs.

Display rotating promotions for add-on services:
- Bath and blowout with daycare ($20-40 add-on)
- Nail trims and ear cleaning
- Training sessions with your in-house trainer
- Enrichment packages (one-on-one playtime, frozen Kongs, puzzle toys)
- Boarding upgrades (private suite, extra walks, bedtime cuddle sessions)

These aren't hard sells — they're reminders. Many clients don't know you offer nail trims until they see it on a screen. A photo of a freshly groomed dog with a "$15 nail trim add-on today" caption is all it takes.

One mid-size daycare in a suburban market reported that adding a single upsell slide to their lobby screen increased bath bookings by 30% in the first month. The screen runs around the clock; your staff doesn't have to say a word.

### 3. Vaccine and Wellness Reminders

Boarding facilities require up-to-date vaccines. Most daycares do too. But clients forget. And chasing down vaccine records is one of the most time-consuming admin tasks at any pet care business.

Use your lobby screen to proactively remind clients:
- "Bordetella vaccine required every 6 months — is yours current?"
- "Flu season is here — ask about the Canine Influenza vaccine"
- "Annual wellness exams help us keep your pup safe — does your vet have current records on file?"

This reduces your admin burden and positions you as a facility that takes health seriously — which is exactly what premium clients want to hear.

### 4. Community Building and Dog Spotlights

Dog parents love their dogs more than most things. Lean into that.

Use your screens to recognize and celebrate:
- **Dog of the Month** — Feature a regular with their name, breed, and a fun fact
- **Birthday shoutouts** — "Happy birthday, Biscuit! 5 years old today!"
- **Milestone moments** — "Scout just hit 100 days of daycare!"
- **New arrivals** — "Welcome to the family, Pretzel!" (with a photo of their first day)

This content costs you almost nothing to create — a quick photo and a slide — but it builds the kind of community loyalty that drives word-of-mouth referrals. When clients see their dog on the screen, they take a photo and post it. That's free marketing.

### 5. Policy and Operational Updates

Facilities change policies, adjust hours, close for holidays, and run promotions. Getting that information to clients is a constant communication challenge.

Your lobby screen handles it automatically:
- Holiday closures and modified hours
- New check-in procedures or forms
- Policy updates (vaccine requirements, new breed restrictions, capacity limits)
- Upcoming events (puppy playdates, open house tours, training workshops)

Update it once from your phone, and every client who walks in sees it. No reprinting, no posting, no relying on staff to remember to mention it.

### 6. Local Advertiser Revenue

Here's where your lobby screen stops being a cost and starts being income.

Your clients — repeat visitors with disposable income who love their pets — are exactly who local businesses want to reach:

- **Veterinary clinics** — Clients who trust you will also trust a vet you endorse. A screen ad from a local vet practice is highly credible in your environment.
- **Pet supply stores and boutiques** — Premium food brands, local toy shops, and specialty pet gear stores want your audience.
- **Dog trainers and behavioral consultants** — Clients asking about training are already considering it. An ad from a local trainer is well-timed.
- **Pet photographers** — Capturing family portraits with pets is a growing service. Your clients are the ideal target.
- **Restaurants and cafes with dog patios** — A meaningful segment of dog owners specifically choose dining spots where their dog is welcome.
- **Dog-friendly hotels and vacation rentals** — People who board their dogs are also the people who vacation with them (or want to).

## Where to Place Screens in a Dog Daycare

| Location | Best Content | Why |
|----------|-------------|-----|
| Main lobby / check-in desk | Play cam, upsells, community posts | First and last thing clients see |
| Waiting area (if separate) | Ads, service promotions, dog spotlights | Longer dwell time, relaxed attention |
| Retail/product area | Product highlights, food recommendations | Purchase decision point |
| Outdoor pickup window (if applicable) | Wait status, wellness reminders | Brief but frequent touchpoint |

Most facilities start with one screen at the front desk and add a second in the waiting area once they see the impact.
