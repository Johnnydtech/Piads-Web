## Customer Behavior and Segments

### CAASie.co Customer Profile

**Who uses CAASie:**

CAASie.co targets small-to-medium businesses (SMBs), startups, marketing agencies, and freelancers who want to buy outdoor billboard advertising without the traditional complexity of media buying. Their platform aggregates 500k+ boards from 40+ publishers across Australia, USA, UK, NZ, EU, and Canada.

_Typical CAASie customer:_
- Marketing manager at a mid-size brand running awareness campaigns
- Agency buyer managing billboard campaigns for multiple clients
- Startup founder launching brand awareness in a new market
- E-commerce brand trying offline channels for the first time

_Source: https://www.getapp.com/marketing-software/a/caasie/, https://caasie.co/en-us_

**CAASie's customer behavior patterns:**

- **Self-serve, one-time campaign mindset.** CAASie customers typically run time-bound campaigns (product launches, events, seasonal pushes) rather than ongoing advertising. The "no contracts, no commitments" positioning attracts transactional buyers.
- **Map-based discovery.** Customers browse a map to find boards by location, format, and venue type, then hand-select individual screens. This favors users who already know what geographic area they want to target.
- **Budget-conscious but broad.** CAASie's pay-per-play pricing ($0.05–$8.50/play) attracts price-sensitive buyers, but the campaigns tend toward mass awareness (highways, transit stations) rather than hyperlocal targeting.
- **Agency workflow.** CAASie offers agency accounts with client segregation, suggesting a significant portion of spend comes through agencies rather than direct SMB buyers.

## Customer Pain Points and Needs

### Pain Point 1: Billboard Advertising Is Too Broad for Hyperlocal Businesses

The fundamental pain point CAASie inherits from its medium: billboards are designed for mass awareness, not hyperlocal conversion. For an Arlington dentist, yoga studio, or tutoring center that serves a 1-5 mile radius, billboard advertising has structural problems:

- **Geographic waste.** A billboard on I-66 or Route 50 near Arlington reaches commuters passing through, not residents who live and spend locally. You're paying for 100,000 impressions when 95,000 of those people will never visit your business.
- **No venue context.** A billboard ad for a massage therapist exists on a highway. A PiAds screen ad for the same therapist exists inside a gym where people with sore muscles are already thinking about recovery. Context transforms advertising from interruption to relevance.
- **3-second attention window.** Billboard viewers are driving at 35-65 mph. The average attention span for a billboard is 3-5 seconds. Venue screen viewers are sitting in a coffee shop for 20-60 minutes or in a salon chair for 30-90 minutes. Dwell time isn't even comparable.
- **No repeat frequency for individuals.** Unless someone drives the same route daily, billboard exposure is random. Venue screens reach the same regulars 15-20 times per month — the frequency required for local brand recall.

_Source: https://oneday.agency/blog/what-are-the-advantages-and-disadvantages-of-billboard-advertising, https://www.totalcommarketing.com/post/are-billboards-effective_

**PiAds advantage:** Every impression is inside a venue the viewer chose to visit, in a corridor where they already spend time. Zero geographic waste.

### Pain Point 2: Rising Digital Ad Costs Are Crushing Local Business ROI

This is the macro pain point driving demand for alternatives to both Google Ads and billboard advertising:

- **68% of businesses feel they wasted money** on digital ads in 2025. $740 billion was spent on digital ads globally, yet most small businesses can't point to clear ROI.
- **Google Ads CPC at all-time highs** across nearly every industry in 2026. Cost-per-click has increased 30-40% over three years. For local services in competitive metro areas like Arlington/DC, CPCs of $4-8 are common.
- **Over 50% of Google Ads budgets are wasted** on poor targeting, broad match "zombie" campaigns, and unoptimized AI settings. Small businesses without dedicated PPC expertise are most affected.
- **Geographic targeting is imprecise.** Google's "Arlington, VA" targeting includes people searching from or about Arlington — not just people physically present and ready to buy in a specific corridor.
- **Clicks ≠ customers.** A 2-3% conversion rate on Google Ads means 97-98% of clicks are wasted. For a local business spending $500/month, that's 5-10 actual customers at $50-100 each.

_Source: https://adskong.com/blog/google-ads-spending-money-but-not-getting-leads-here-s-the-2026-fix, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/steven-mikellides_2025-year-end-review-what-this-year-taught-activity-7407789111710740480-7Coa, https://saltmarketing.ie/google-ads-in-2026-what-to-stop-doing-immediately/_

**PiAds advantage:** $50-75/week for a venue screen that delivers 200-400 daily impressions to people who are physically in your corridor. No clicks to waste. No CPC inflation. No algorithm to fight.

**CAASie partial solution:** CAASie's self-service model is cheaper than traditional billboard buying, but it still operates at billboard scale and pricing. Urban panels start at $0.07/play (~$4.90/hour), billboards at $0.20/play (~$26/hour). A week-long campaign on a single board easily costs $200-500+. Still too broad and expensive for a hyperlocal service business.

### Pain Point 3: Venue Owners Have No Good Content Management Solution

This is the pain point CAASie cannot address at all — it's advertiser-only, demand-side only. Venue owners face:

- **Content creation paralysis.** "I had no idea how to map out content duration and sequencing" is a common sentiment. Without a designer, "professional" becomes "impossible." Operators spend hours in unfamiliar design tools, produce mediocre results, and abandon the effort.
- **Stale screen syndrome.** Screens end up displaying "stale, generic slideshows that audiences tune out" or cable news nobody watches. The TV becomes wasted real estate.
- **Technical overwhelm.** Enterprise digital signage platforms (BrightSign, Scala, Four Winds) are designed for chains and franchises. Monthly software costs of $20-50+ per screen, complex CMS dashboards, and enterprise contracts are overkill for a single coffee shop.
- **No revenue path.** A venue owner with a TV on the wall has no way to monetize that screen. They don't know local advertisers exist who'd pay for screen time, and they don't have a marketplace to connect supply with demand.

_Source: https://seenlabs.com/blog/content-scheduling-burden, https://mvix.com/blog/overcoming-common-challenges/, https://www.crowntv-us.com/blog/11-digital-signage-questions-business-owners-ask-too-late/_

**PiAds advantage:** Free CMS for venue owners. Upload images/videos, set daypart schedules, done. No enterprise pricing, no design expertise needed. Canva templates work perfectly. And when ready, opt into the marketplace to earn 75% of ad revenue — a monetization path no other platform offers at this scale.

**CAASie gap:** CAASie has zero offering for venue owners. It's a demand-side platform that aggregates existing publisher inventory (Clear Channel, Lamar, JCDecaux). It doesn't help a coffee shop owner manage their own screen or earn from it.

### Pain Point 4: Traditional DOOH Platforms Ignore Small Indoor Venues

The traditional DOOH supply chain was built for large-format outdoor media:

- **Publisher networks focus on scale.** Clear Channel, Lamar, and JCDecaux own highway billboards, bus shelters, and transit screens. They don't put screens in local coffee shops because the economics don't work at their scale.
- **Minimum viable venue size is too high.** Traditional DOOH networks require venues with thousands of daily impressions to justify installation and management overhead. A neighborhood gym with 200 daily members doesn't qualify.
- **No self-install model.** Traditional DOOH requires vendor-installed hardware, managed software, and revenue share agreements designed for large venues. The overhead per screen makes small venues unprofitable.
- **CAASie aggregates these same publishers.** CAASie's 500k+ boards come from the same publishers who ignore small indoor venues. Using CAASie doesn't create new indoor inventory — it just makes existing outdoor inventory easier to buy.

**PiAds advantage:** PiAds flips the model. Any venue with a TV and a $30 Fire TV Stick becomes a screen. Self-install in 10 minutes. Free software. No minimum impressions. The venue owner controls the screen and optionally earns from ads. This creates inventory that literally doesn't exist in CAASie's network.

### Pain Point 5: No Trust Layer in Billboard Advertising

For local businesses, trust is everything. And billboards have no trust mechanism:

- **Billboard ads are anonymous.** A viewer sees a brand name on a highway with no context for whether that business is trustworthy, local, or relevant to them.
- **Venue screens carry implicit endorsement.** When a dentist's ad appears inside a coffee shop a customer visits daily, the venue's relationship with the customer transfers to the advertiser. It feels like a recommendation, not an ad.
- **Community matters in Arlington.** Arlington's business culture is relationship-driven. Chamber of Commerce connections, neighborhood loyalty, and word-of-mouth are primary channels. A billboard feels corporate. A venue screen feels like a neighbor's recommendation.

**PiAds advantage:** Every ad is implicitly endorsed by the venue displaying it. Venue owners review and approve ads, ensuring quality and relevance. This trust layer doesn't exist in CAASie's model.

### Market Differentiation

**PiAds' five structural differentiators — none of which any current competitor can easily replicate:**

**1. Two-Sided Platform Architecture**
Every competitor is either demand-side (CAASie, Blip, AdQuick — advertisers buy screen time) or supply-side (Atmosphere TV — provides content to venues). PiAds is the only platform serving both sides: venue CMS AND advertiser marketplace. This creates network effects: every new venue adds inventory, every new advertiser makes venue participation more profitable.

**2. Venue-Owner Revenue Share (75/25)**
Atmosphere TV gives venues free streaming but zero ad revenue. Traditional DOOH publishers keep all ad revenue. PiAds gives 75% of ad revenue to venue owners — turning a cost center (cable subscription) into a revenue stream. This is the single most powerful venue acquisition lever in the market.

**3. Community Trust Layer**
No DOOH platform has a trust mechanism. Billboard ads are anonymous. Atmosphere TV ads are from national brands with no local connection. PiAds ads are implicitly endorsed by the venue displaying them — a trust transfer that converts billboard-style awareness into neighbor-level recommendation.

**4. Hyperlocal Corridor Targeting**
CAASie targets by city or geofence. Blip targets by billboard location. PiAds targets by specific venue in a specific corridor — "your ad at Northside Social on Wilson Blvd during the morning coffee rush." This building-level precision doesn't exist in any competitor's model.

**5. BYOD Hardware Model**
Atmosphere TV sends proprietary devices. Traditional signage requires BrightSign ($300+) or commercial displays. PiAds works with a $30 Fire TV Stick or any Raspberry Pi — hardware most venues already own or can buy at Best Buy. This eliminates the #1 barrier to venue adoption (hardware cost and installation complexity).

### Summary of Key Findings

**CAASie is not PiAds' competitor — it validates that PiAds' market is underserved.**

CAASie proves that self-serve advertising platforms can attract SMB advertisers who are frustrated with traditional media buying complexity. But CAASie's outdoor billboard model has fundamental limitations for hyperlocal businesses: geographic waste, no venue context, 3-second attention windows, and no community trust layer.

PiAds addresses every limitation of the billboard advertising model while creating an entirely new category: **community-owned indoor venue screens with a local advertising marketplace.**

### Strategic Impact Assessment

1. **PiAds has no direct competitor.** No platform combines venue CMS + local advertiser marketplace + community-first positioning + BYOD hardware. This is a genuine whitespace opportunity.

2. **The market timing is ideal.** Rising Google Ads costs, growing DOOH adoption, Arlington BID investment in small business programs, and 31% remote work rates creating daytime venue traffic all converge in PiAds' favor.
